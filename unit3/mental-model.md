# Unit 3 — Mental Model: Cyber-Physical Systems

---

## Topic Groups

### Group 1: Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS) — Core Concepts
**Core idea:** CPS is the tight integration of physical processes with computer-based monitoring and control in a continuous feedback loop. The physical world and the digital world are not separate — they influence each other in real time.

Covers: Q1, Q8, Q13, Q23, Q25, Q27, Q29

---

### Group 2: Next-Generation Sensors
**Core idea:** Modern industrial sensors go far beyond simple on/off signals — they are smart, networked, multi-parameter devices that provide high-resolution, real-time data for CPS and AI systems.

Covers: Q2, Q7, Q12, Q14, Q22

---

### Group 3: AR and VR in Industrial Contexts
**Core idea:** Augmented Reality (AR) overlays digital information onto the real world; Virtual Reality (VR) creates fully immersive simulations. Both are powerful tools for industrial training, maintenance guidance, and quality inspection.

Covers: Q3, Q4, Q6, Q11, Q18, Q19, Q26

---

### Group 4: AI, Big Data & Advanced Analytics in CPS
**Core idea:** AI and Big Data transform the raw sensor streams from CPS into intelligent decisions. AI detects patterns, optimizes processes, and makes autonomous decisions; Big Data provides the infrastructure to store and process industrial-scale data volumes.

Covers: Q15, Q20, Q21, Q24, Q28

---

### Group 5: PLM, Collaborative Platforms & Digital Integration
**Core idea:** Product Lifecycle Management (PLM) manages a product's entire life from design to disposal using digital tools. Collaborative platforms enable real-time information sharing between teams, departments, and organizations across the value chain.

Covers: Q9, Q10, Q16, Q17
