# Unit 1: Introduction to Industrial IoT (IIoT) Systems

## Low Difficulty – 20%
1. Define Industry 4.0.
2. What is meant by Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT)?
3. List the four industrial revolutions.
4. State any two benefits of Industry 4.0.

## Moderate Difficulty – 60%
5. Explain the evolution from Industry 1.0 to Industry 4.0.
6. Describe the role of IoT in modern manufacturing industries.
7. Differentiate between IoT and IIoT.
8. Explain the architecture of an Industry 4.0 system.
9. Discuss cyber-physical integration in smart factories.
10. Explain the importance of interoperability in Industry 4.0.
11. Describe the role of data in Industry 4.0 ecosystems.
12. Explain organizational support required for Industry 4.0 adoption.
13. Explain technological enablers of Industry 4.0.
14. Discuss challenges in implementing Industry 4.0.
15. Illustrate smart manufacturing with a suitable example.
16. Explain the concept of vertical and horizontal integration.

## High Difficulty – 20%
17. Apply the concepts of different industrial revolutions to explain how a traditional manufacturing unit can be transformed into an Industry 4.0-based smart factory.
18. Propose strategies to overcome Industry 4.0 implementation challenges.
19. Apply the principles of Internet of Things (IoT) and Industrial IoT (IIoT) to design a system for predictive maintenance in an industrial setup.
20. Design a conceptual Industry 4.0 model for an automated plant.
21. Analyze the impact of Industry 4.0 on workforce skills.
22. Evaluate the readiness of Indian industries for Industry 4.0 adoption.
23. Propose strategies to overcome Industry 4.0 implementation challenges.
