# Unit 1 — Mental Model: Introduction to Industrial IoT (IIoT) Systems

---

## Topic Groups

### Group 1: Industrial Revolutions & Industry 4.0 Basics
**Core idea:** Understanding the historical progression of industrial revolutions and how Industry 4.0 emerges as the latest transformation — fusing digital, physical, and biological systems.

Covers: Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4, Q5, Q17, Q21

---

### Group 2: IoT vs IIoT
**Core idea:** IoT connects everyday devices to the internet; IIoT applies this concept specifically in industrial environments with higher reliability, security, and precision requirements.

Covers: Q2, Q6, Q7, Q19

---

### Group 3: Industry 4.0 Architecture & Integration
**Core idea:** Industry 4.0 operates through layered architectures — physical devices connect upward through networks to cloud/AI layers. Vertical and horizontal integration describe how these layers connect within and across enterprise boundaries.

Covers: Q8, Q9, Q16, Q20

---

### Group 4: Technological Enablers & Smart Manufacturing
**Core idea:** A set of key technologies (AI, Big Data, Cloud, Cyber-Physical Systems, Robotics, IoT, AR/VR) powers Industry 4.0. Smart manufacturing is the practical implementation of these in a production environment.

Covers: Q13, Q15, Q20

---

### Group 5: Challenges, Organizational Readiness & Strategy
**Core idea:** Implementing Industry 4.0 requires addressing technological, human, financial, and regulatory challenges. Organizational support, workforce upskilling, and interoperability are critical success factors.

Covers: Q10, Q11, Q12, Q14, Q18, Q22, Q23
